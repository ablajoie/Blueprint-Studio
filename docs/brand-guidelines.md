# Blueprint Studio Brand Foundation

Blueprint Studio should feel precise, capable, and approachable: a professional design workspace for people who understand Salesforce solutions but do not want the friction of a high-fidelity design tool.

## Mark

The Blueprint Studio mark is a geometric **B** built from a drafting spine and two metadata blocks. The structure represents the product's core promise: turn solution intent into organized, connected metadata.

- Use the full lockup (mark plus “Blueprint Studio”) in application headers and product introductions.
- Use the mark alone for the browser favicon, compact navigation, and square app icons.
- Keep clear space around the mark equal to at least one quarter of its width.
- Do not place the mark below 24 px in product UI.
- Do not redraw, rotate, stretch, or add effects to the mark.

The vector favicon is stored at `public/favicon.svg`. The in-app mark is rendered by the reusable `BrandMark` component so it remains crisp at every supported size.

## Color palette

| Role            | Color                                 | Hex       |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | --------- |
| Blueprint Navy  | Primary brand surface and header      | `#0B2341` |
| Studio Blue     | Actions and drafting node             | `#2F6FED` |
| Signal Cyan     | Connections, focus, and highlights    | `#22C7D6` |
| Blueprint Paper | Quiet application background          | `#F5F9FF` |
| White           | Mark structure and high-contrast text | `#FFFFFF` |

Use navy as the anchor, blue for primary actions, and cyan sparingly for focus and connection cues. The palette is Salesforce-familiar without copying Salesforce brand assets or trade dress.

## Typography and voice

Continue using the application's system sans-serif stack for speed, clarity, and native rendering. Product language should be concise and encouraging:

- Prefer “Let's add your first object” over “No objects found.”
- Explain the next useful action without turning the interface into a wizard.
- Describe design readiness as guidance, not punishment.
- Use Salesforce terminology when it improves recognition; use plain language everywhere else.

## Product expression

The brand should reinforce four qualities:

1. **Structured** — metadata and decisions have a clear home.
2. **Connected** — artifacts expose relationships and impact.
3. **Practical** — workflows reflect real BA and architect work.
4. **Calm** — complexity is progressively disclosed instead of shown all at once.
